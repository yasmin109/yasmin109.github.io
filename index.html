About

I'm a Computer Science student who likes taking a problem apart — whether that's a disease-outbreak dataset or a blockchain integrity check — until the solution is simple enough to explain to anyone.

My background sits at the intersection of applied data analysis and secure systems engineering. During my internship at Vulture Lines Tech Management, I worked with Python to build a disease outbreak prediction pipeline — my first real exposure to using data science for public-good outcomes. That experience shaped what I look for now: problems where technical rigor actually changes an outcome for someone.

Outside coursework, I've represented my team at the Design & Innovation Clinic 2026 in Bengaluru, presenting a fingerprint-based secure data storage system with blockchain integrity verification — work that pushed me deeper into cryptographic security and distributed systems, two areas I want to keep building in.
